Notation Guide

This guide covers the symbols, conventions, and notation styles used throughout LogicVault for Boolean algebra and digital logic.

Boolean Operators

#
Symbol Operation Alternative Notations Example
· AND (Conjunction) ∧, &, ×, AB (implied) A · B or AB
+ OR (Disjunction) ∨, |, || A + B
A̅ NOT (Complement) ¬A, A', !A, ~A A̅ or A'
⊕ XOR (Exclusive OR) ^, ⊻ A ⊕ B
⊙ XNOR (Equivalence) ↔, ⊙ A ⊙ B
↑ NAND (A · B)̅ A ↑ B
↓ NOR (A + B)̅ A ↓ B

Precedence (highest to lowest): NOT → AND → XOR → OR. Use parentheses to override: (A + B) · C

Variables & Constants

#

Boolean variables are typically represented by uppercase letters. Common conventions:

A, B, CGeneric inputs
X, Y, ZIntermediate/output
F, QFunction output
0, 1Logic low/high
ConstantMeaningAlso Called
0Logic LowFALSE, L, GND
1Logic HighTRUE, H, VCC

Complement notation: Throughout LogicVault, we use the overbar (A̅) as the primary notation for complement, with A' as an alternative.

Expression Styles

#

Mathematical

F = A̅B + AB̅
Overbar for complement, implicit AND

Programming

F = (!A & B) | (A & !B)
C/Verilog style operators

Explicit Operators

F = A' · B + A · B'
Prime notation, explicit dot

Set Notation

F = A̅ ∧ B ∨ A ∧ B̅
Logic symbols (∧, ∨, ¬)
Example: XOR function in different notations
A ⊕ B = A̅B + AB̅ = (A + B)(A̅ + B̅)
Programming: (A ^ B) = (!A & B) | (A & !B)

Standard Forms

#
FormDescriptionExample
SOP Sum of Products — OR of AND terms (minterms) F = A̅B̅C + A̅BC + ABC
POS Product of Sums — AND of OR terms (maxterms) F = (A+B+C)(A+B̅+C)
Minterm Product term where every variable appears once m₃ = A̅BC (for ABC=011)
Maxterm Sum term where every variable appears once M₃ = A+B̅+C̅ (for ABC=011)
Compact notation using Σ and Π
F(A,B,C) = Σm(0,2,5,7) = Πм(1,3,4,6)
Sum of minterms 0,2,5,7 = Product of maxterms 1,3,4,6

Don't care conditions are represented by d or X: F(A,B,C) = Σm(1,3,5) + d(2,6)
