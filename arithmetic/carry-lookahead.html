Generate and Propagate Signals

#

The key insight of CLA is that for each bit position, we can determine two things:

Generate (G)

A carry is generated at position i when both inputs are 1. This creates a carry regardless of any incoming carry.

Gi = Ai · Bi
Propagate (P)

A carry is propagated through position i when exactly one input is 1. An incoming carry passes through to the next stage.

Pi = Ai ⊕ Bi

Carry equation (fundamental CLA recurrence)

Ci+1 = Gi + (Pi · Ci)

Sum equation

Sumi = Pi ⊕ Ci

Key insight: Pi is the same as the half-adder sum (XOR). The propagate signal tells us "if a carry comes in, it will go out."

Expanded Carry Equations (4-bit CLA)

#

By recursively expanding the carry equation, we can express each carry in terms of G, P, and C0 only:

4-bit CLA Carry Equations

C1 = G0 + P0·C0

C2 = G1 + P1·G0 + P1·P0·C0

C3 = G2 + P2·G1 + P2·P1·G0 + P2·P1·P0·C0

C4 = G3 + P3·G2 + P3·P2·G1 + P3·P2·P1·G0 + P3·P2·P1·P0·C0

Group Generate and Propagate (for hierarchical CLA)

Ggroup = G3 + P3·G2 + P3·P2·G1 + P3·P2·P1·G0

Pgroup = P3 · P2 · P1 · P0

Fan-in limitation: Each carry equation requires increasingly wide AND/OR gates. For C4, we need a 5-input OR gate. This limits practical single-level CLA to 4 bits. Larger adders use hierarchical (multi-level) CLA.

RTL Implementation

#
Verilog cla_4bit.v 
// 4-bit Carry Lookahead Adder
module cla_4bit (
    input  [3:0] a, b,
    input        cin,
    output [3:0] sum,
    output       cout,
    output       pg, gg  // Group P and G for hierarchical CLA
);

    wire [3:0] p, g;
    wire [4:0] c;

    // Generate P and G for each bit
    assign p = a ^ b;  // Propagate
    assign g = a & b;  // Generate

    // Carry Lookahead Logic
    assign c[0] = cin;
    assign c[1] = g[0] | (p[0] & c[0]);
    assign c[2] = g[1] | (p[1] & g[0]) | (p[1] & p[0] & c[0]);
    assign c[3] = g[2] | (p[2] & g[1]) | (p[2] & p[1] & g[0]) | (p[2] & p[1] & p[0] & c[0]);
    assign c[4] = g[3] | (p[3] & g[2]) | (p[3] & p[2] & g[1]) | (p[3] & p[2] & p[1] & g[0]) | (p[3] & p[2] & p[1] & p[0] & c[0]);

    // Sum computation
    assign sum = p ^ c[3:0];
    assign cout = c[4];

    // Group signals for hierarchical CLA
    assign pg = p[3] & p[2] & p[1] & p[0];
    assign gg = g[3] | (p[3] & g[2]) | (p[3] & p[2] & g[1]) | (p[3] & p[2] & p[1] & g[0]);

endmodule
VHDL cla_4bit.vhd 
library IEEE;
use IEEE.STD_LOGIC_1164.all;

entity cla_4bit is
    port (
        a, b : in  std_logic_vector(3 downto 0);
        cin   : in  std_logic;
        sum   : out std_logic_vector(3 downto 0);
        cout  : out std_logic;
        pg, gg: out std_logic
    );
end cla_4bit;

architecture dataflow of cla_4bit is
    signal p, g : std_logic_vector(3 downto 0);
    signal c    : std_logic_vector(4 downto 0);
begin
    p <= a xor b;
    g <= a and b;
    
    c(0) <= cin;
    c(1) <= g(0) or (p(0) and c(0));
    c(2) <= g(1) or (p(1) and g(0)) or (p(1) and p(0) and c(0));
    c(3) <= g(2) or (p(2) and g(1)) or (p(2) and p(1) and g(0)) 
            or (p(2) and p(1) and p(0) and c(0));
    c(4) <= g(3) or (p(3) and g(2)) or (p(3) and p(2) and g(1))
            or (p(3) and p(2) and p(1) and g(0))
            or (p(3) and p(2) and p(1) and p(0) and c(0));
    
    sum  <= p xor c(3 downto 0);
    cout <= c(4);
    pg   <= p(3) and p(2) and p(1) and p(0);
    gg   <= g(3) or (p(3) and g(2)) or (p(3) and p(2) and g(1))
            or (p(3) and p(2) and p(1) and g(0));
end dataflow;

Interview Notes

#
  • What's the difference between P and G? — Generate (G = A·B) creates a carry unconditionally. Propagate (P = A⊕B) passes an incoming carry to the next stage.
  • Why is CLA faster than RCA? — CLA computes all carries in parallel using combinational logic, while RCA waits for carry to ripple through each stage sequentially.
  • What limits single-level CLA to ~4 bits? — Fan-in constraints. C4 needs a 5-input OR gate, and gate delays increase with fan-in. Larger CLAs use hierarchical (multi-level) design.
  • How do you build a 16-bit CLA? — Use four 4-bit CLA blocks with a second-level CLU that computes carries between groups using group P and G signals.
  • What's the delay of an N-bit hierarchical CLA? — O(log N). Specifically, ~4 + 2⌈log₄N⌉ gate delays for N bits using 4-bit blocks.
