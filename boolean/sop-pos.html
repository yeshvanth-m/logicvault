SOP and POS Forms

Sum of Products (SOP) and Product of Sums (POS) are the two standard canonical forms for Boolean expressions. Understanding both is essential for logic minimization and circuit implementation.

SOP vs POS Comparison

#

Sum of Products (SOP)

Also called: Disjunctive Normal Form (DNF)

F = (A·B) + (A'·C) + (B·C')
  • OR (+) of AND (·) terms
  • Each AND term is a product term
  • Uses minterms (m)
  • Notation: F = Σm(1,3,5,7)
  • K-Map: group the 1s

Product of Sums (POS)

Also called: Conjunctive Normal Form (CNF)

F = (A+B) · (A'+C) · (B+C')
  • AND (·) of OR (+) terms
  • Each OR term is a sum term
  • Uses maxterms (M)
  • Notation: F = ΠM(0,2,4,6)
  • K-Map: group the 0s

Minterms & Maxterms

#

For n variables, there are 2ⁿ minterms and 2ⁿ maxterms. Each row of a truth table corresponds to one minterm and one maxterm.

A B C Decimal Minterm (m) Maxterm (M)
0000 A'B'C' A+B+C
0011 A'B'C A+B+C'
0102 A'BC' A+B'+C
0113 A'BC A+B'+C'
1004 AB'C' A'+B+C
1015 AB'C A'+B+C'
1106 ABC' A'+B'+C
1117 ABC A'+B'+C'

Pattern: Minterm uses the variable as-is for 1, complemented for 0. Maxterm is the opposite — complemented for 1, as-is for 0.

Canonical SOP: Sum the minterms where F=1

F = Σm(1,3,4,6)
F = A'B'C + A'BC + AB'C' + ABC'

Canonical POS: Product the maxterms where F=0

F = ΠM(0,2,5,7)
F = (A+B+C)(A+B'+C)(A'+B+C')(A'+B'+C')

Form Conversion

#

Converting between SOP and POS is straightforward using complements:

SOP ↔ POS Conversion:

If F = Σm(1,3,4,6) → F = ΠM(0,2,5,7)
// Maxterm indices = all indices NOT in minterm list

For 3 variables (indices 0-7):
Minterms: {1,3,4,6} → Maxterms: {0,2,5,7}

Quick rule: SOP uses minterms where output is 1. POS uses maxterms where output is 0. Together they cover all 2ⁿ rows.

Gate Implementation

#

Each form maps naturally to a two-level gate structure:

SOP: Inverters → AND gates → OR gate
SOP (NAND): NAND → NAND (using De Morgan)
POS: Inverters → OR gates → AND gate
POS (NOR): NOR → NOR (using De Morgan)

NAND-NAND = AND-OR: Because (AB)' followed by ((AB)'·(CD)')' = AB + CD. This is why NAND-only implementations are preferred in CMOS.
